Your Escondido Junk Removal Team

Escondido — Spanish for "hidden" — sits about 30 miles north of downtown San Diego in the San Pasqual Valley, ringed by avocado groves, citrus orchards, and the chaparral hills of the North County backcountry. The city has about 150,000 residents and a housing mix that runs from Downtown's historic Grand Avenue district to the agricultural-heritage homes in Felicita and the newer hillside communities in Hidden Meadows and Eagle Crest. The agricultural history means a lot of properties here have outbuildings, sheds, and decades of accumulated yard equipment — different junk profile than coastal San Diego.

From our San Diego base it's a 35–40 minute drive up I-15 to reach central Escondido, so we treat North County Inland as a route, not a one-off trip. We run trucks through here multiple days per week, which usually lets us hit same-day or next-day pickup windows.

Neighborhoods we regularly service in Escondido: Downtown / Grand Avenue, Old Escondido, Felicita, Eagle Crest, Hidden Meadows, North Broadway, the Kit Carson Park area, San Pasqual Valley, Harmony Grove, and the rural pockets toward Valley Center.

Zip codes covered: 92025, 92026, 92027, 92029 — flat pricing across all four, including the larger-lot agricultural properties.

Where Your Junk Goes

Responsible Disposal for Escondido Loads

Escondido loads route to one of two facilities we use across every job: the Miramar Landfill in Kearny Mesa (about a 25–30 minute drive south on I-15) for general waste and recyclables, or the Sycamore Landfill in Santee (about a 30-minute drive southeast) for heavier inert and demolition material. We typically batch multiple North County jobs into a single route, so disposal happens at the end of the day rather than between every pickup — which is why our two-hour job windows hold even with the longer drive back to base.

Yard waste and brush from defensible-space clearance is composted, not landfilled, when material is clean and trimmings stay under 4 inches in diameter. For estate cleanouts in long-tenure Escondido homes, we'll separate usable furniture and household items for North County donation partners — flag this at booking if you want a tax-deductible drop-off receipt.
