How Much Does Junk Removal Cost in San Diego?

Pricing for junk removal in San Diego isn't one-size-fits-all — but it's also not a mystery. Here's an honest, straightforward breakdown of what you can expect to pay based on load size, item type, and job complexity.

The Short Answer

Most junk removal jobs in San Diego start at $99 and range up to $900+ depending on how much we haul. Where you land depends on three things: how much space your junk takes up in the truck, how heavy it is, and whether any special disposal fees apply.

What Actually Drives the Price?

1. Volume (The Biggest Factor)

Junk removal is priced primarily by how much space your stuff takes up in the truck. Think of it like renting storage — you pay for the cubic footage, not the number of items. A single heavy armoire might cost more than ten cardboard boxes because it takes up more truck space.

2. Weight

Dump facilities charge by the ton, and those fees get passed along. Items like concrete, dirt, roofing materials, and tile are heavy and expensive to dispose of. An 800-lb pile of broken concrete is going to cost more than an 800-lb pile of furniture, because concrete disposal fees are higher.

3. Special Disposal Items

Some items have mandatory disposal fees in California — including mattresses (California Mattress Recycling Fee), televisions and monitors (e-waste fees), and appliances containing refrigerant (Freon recovery). These fees are typically small ($15–$30 per item) but honest companies will tell you upfront.

4. Labor & Accessibility

If your items are in a basement, up a flight of stairs, or require disassembly before removal, expect the price to reflect the extra labor. Moving a washer/dryer down a narrow staircase takes real effort — that's priced accordingly.

What's NOT Included in the Price

Reputable junk removal companies in San Diego give you an all-in price before any work starts. Be cautious of companies that don't disclose their pricing model upfront. Hidden charges to watch for include:

  • Fuel surcharges added after the fact
  • Separate dump fees billed separately from the quoted price
  • "Trip fees" for multiple hauls not disclosed upfront

At SD Hauling & Demo, we give you one upfront number that covers everything — labor, fuel, truck, and disposal. You only pay when the job is done.

Is Junk Removal Cheaper Than Renting a Dumpster?

Dumpster rentals in San Diego typically run $350–$650 for a 10-yard container with a few days of rental time. That might make sense if you're doing a long DIY cleanout over several days. But for most homeowners doing a one-day purge, full-service junk removal is often the same price or cheaper — and you don't have to do any of the lifting yourself.

Yes. Our minimum charge is $99. This covers the cost of sending out a truck and crew, fuel, and disposal fees — even for very small jobs. It's the baseline cost of doing the work right.

Do you charge by the item or by the truckload?

We price by volume — how much space your junk takes up in the truck. This is the fairest model for most customers because two couches take up more space than five small boxes. For very small jobs (1–3 items), we sometimes price per item, but we always give you the final number before we start.
