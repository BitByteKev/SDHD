FAQ

Demolition Services FAQs

Common questions about our demolition and debris removal service.

Do I need a permit for shed removal in San Diego?

Most residential shed removals don't require a permit, particularly for structures under 120 square feet. However, requirements vary by city and HOA. Our D-63 licensed crew handles the full removal and debris cleanup — contact us and we'll help clarify for your specific situation.

How much does demolition cost in San Diego?

Demolition costs vary by project scope. Shed removals typically start at $300–$500, deck demolitions range from $400–$800, and interior demo projects are priced by scope of work. All quotes are free and upfront — no surprises.

Are you licensed for demolition in California?

Yes. We hold a D-63 Post Construction & Demolition Cleanup certification and CA Contractor License #1135398. We are fully licensed, bonded, and insured for all demolition work throughout San Diego County.

Do you handle concrete breaking and removal?

Yes. We break and remove concrete slabs, driveways, patios, and sidewalks. Our crew uses professional equipment to break up the concrete and hauls all debris away in one trip — leaving the site clean and ready.

How long does interior demolition take?

A single-room interior demo typically takes 1–2 days. Larger multi-room projects may take several days depending on scope. We'll give you a realistic timeline and upfront quote before starting any work.
